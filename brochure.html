The Amazing Theory of Holism

Lance Robert Stringer

One hundred years ago, the statesman, scientist, and philosopher Jan Christiaan Smuts introduced the word “holism” to the English language in his landmark book Holism and Evolution. His theory was bold: that the Universe has an inherent tendency to form increasingly integrated and complex wholes — and that the causal powers of those wholes exceed the sum of their parts.

Although limited to the science of his day, Smuts proved to be spectacularly far-seeing. Today, across the biological sciences and quantum theory alike, modern research has vindicated him. His insights were not mystical notions but empirical observations about how the Universe actually operates.
